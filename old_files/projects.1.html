Data Centre Caithness Renewable
49 MW IT Load

Forss Data Centre

Forss Energy & Business Park, Caithness, Scotland

The Forss Data Centre is a 49 MW IT load colocation campus being developed by FTP at Forss Energy & Business Park, approximately 14 kilometres west of Thurso. The site benefits from proximity to former Dounreay nuclear infrastructure, Scotland's highest-quality onshore wind resource, and a North Atlantic climate enabling extended free-cooling periods of 6,500–7,500 hours per annum.

The project is structured in two phases: Phase 1 delivers 20 MW of IT load targeting commercial operation in Q1 2029, with full 49 MW build-out completing by Q3 2031. The facility is designed to Tier III+ certification (Uptime Institute) with a Tier IV design intent, a PUE target of ≤1.10, and WUE of ≤0.5 L/kWh — powered entirely by renewable energy via a long-term PPA.

An SPV — Forss Data Centre Limited — is being incorporated in Scotland to develop, finance, construct, and operate the project. FTP is the developer and is actively seeking anchor colocation tenants and project finance.

Energy Recovery CHP Freeport
25 MW Electrical

Port Nigg Energy Recovery Facility

Port Nigg Freeport Zone 1, Cromarty Firth, Scotland

The Port Nigg ERF is a proposed 25 MW combined heat and power Energy Recovery Facility to be developed within the Port Nigg Freeport boundary on the Cromarty Firth. The facility is designed to address a critical power constraint at the port — current grid transmission capacity of approximately 8–10 MW is insufficient to support the energy demands of incoming industrial tenants, particularly those supporting FLOW offshore wind manufacturing.

The ERF provides non-intermittent, dispatchable baseload electricity via a private wire arrangement directly to port tenants, decoupling Port Nigg's industrial development from SSEN grid upgrade timelines. In addition to 25 MW of electrical output, the plant can deliver up to 40 MWth of thermal energy, creating optional integration with co-located industrial users.

The project uses proven moving-grate technology and is designed to process 300,000–350,000 tonnes per annum of residual waste feedstock. FTP is developing the project under a DBF(O)T — Design, Build, Finance, Operate, Transfer — model, targeting first power in 2029.
